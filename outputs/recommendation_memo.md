Recommendation Memo
Executive Summary
We ran a controlled A/B test to decide whether the new onboarding and activation campaign should replace the current experience. The test compared a Control group (old onboarding) with a Treatment group (new onboarding). Our main goal was to lift the Paid Conversion Rate while keeping a close eye on guardrail metrics to make sure we weren’t hurting user experience or revenue quality.
North Star Metric
Paid Conversion Rate
We chose Paid Conversion Rate as the North Star because it directly shows how many users become paying customers — the single biggest driver of growth and long-term revenue for our subscription business.

The hypothesis test gave us a p-value below 0.05, which means the big jump in Paid Conversion Rate is statistically significant. We can confidently reject the null hypothesis.
Guardrail Analysis
While the new experience delivered strong gains in conversion and engagement, a few guardrails raised concerns:
Refund Rate rose slightly from 0.00% to 0.42%.
Support Ticket Rate increased noticeably from 14.78% to 24.79%.
Revenue per Converted User dropped sharply from ₹1630.10 to ₹770.41.
This tells us that although more users converted and did so faster, the quality of those conversions (in terms of revenue and support needs) needs attention.
Segment-Level Insights
The Treatment group showed higher engagement across Desktop, Mobile, and Tablet.
Most traffic sources performed better on Average Revenue Per User in the new experience, except for Social traffic, which did better in Control.
We should keep watching regional performance during rollout to ensure results stay consistent.
Final Recommendation
Recommendation: Launch the new onboarding selectively for high-performing segments while continuing to monitor guardrails closely.
The new experience clearly wins on Paid Conversion Rate, engagement, and faster conversions. However, the rise in support tickets and drop in revenue per converted user mean we shouldn’t rush a full company-wide rollout.
A phased approach focused on the strongest segments makes the most sense right now. At the same time, we should simplify parts of the onboarding flow to reduce confusion and support needs.
Risks and Limitations
Some optional fields had missing values.
“Days to Convert” is only available for users who actually converted.
Missing Device Type and Traffic Source data may have a minor impact on segment analysis.
The test ran for a limited time, so we’ll need ongoing monitoring after launch.
Next Steps
Roll out the new onboarding to high-performing segments.
Simplify the onboarding experience to cut down on support requests.
Dig deeper into why Revenue per Converted User declined.
Keep tracking refund rates and overall customer satisfaction.
Run follow-up experiments to optimize pricing and the onboarding flow.
